The Unkept Keepsakes

Francheska Louise S. Estiller

How often will I think of you?... I'll mend, I suppose.

Your outline will blur, the shape of you will soften,
but you will never completely fade.
Maybe I'll hear your laugh in half-remembered jokes
or think of you whenever I’m faced with a bowl of sinigang.
Every so often, your favorite song will find me,
and I’ll hear your voice threaded through its notes.

I'll know your favorite color before I even see it;
and that green striped shirt you wore so often,
it’s stitched into the fabric of my memory.
Fields of yellow flowers will betray me,
because with them will come the memory
of sunlight carving your face into gold.

Before night finally takes me,
I'll carry the hush of your breathing as you slept;
and when morning breaks,
I'll remember the way your dreams came alive in your voice,
before the world had the chance to touch them.

Some things that haunt me now will remain,
like keepsakes I cannot bring myself to throw away.
But I will learn to live around the echoes,
to let them breathe inside the rhythm of my days.
Not healed so much as rearranged:
you a quiet room I sometimes return to,
and myself, learning... how to leave the door open.
